The Power of Better Prompts
‚è±Ô∏è 10 min
Objective: Learn how prompt quality dramatically affects output quality by comparing vague vs. specific prompts.

Part A: The Vague Prompt

Weak Prompt
Write a marketing email.

Send this prompt and note the result. It will likely be generic and not very useful.

Part B: The Specific Prompt

Strong Prompt (CRAFT Method)
Context: I run a small accounting firm launching a new service for small business tax planning. Role: Act as an experienced B2B email copywriter. Action: Write a marketing email to small business owners (10-50 employees) announcing our new quarterly tax planning service. Format: - Subject line (compelling, under 50 characters) - Email body (150-200 words) - Clear call-to-action for a free consultation Tone: Professional but approachable, emphasising value and expertise without being pushy.

Compare the Results

Aspect Vague Prompt Result Specific Prompt Result
Relevance to your needs
Ready to use as-is?
Tone appropriateness
Time to get useful output
The CRAFT Framework

Context - Background information
Role - Who the AI should act as
Action - What you want done
Format - How to structure the output
Tone - Voice and style

üéÅ Bonus: Advanced Prompting Techniques

Chain of Thought Prompting

Ask AI to "think step by step" for complex problems:

"Let's think through this step by step: [your complex question]"
Role-Based Prompting

Assign a specific expert role for better domain responses:

"Act as an experienced [role] with 20 years of experience. [Your question]"
Iterative Refinement

Don't accept the first response - refine it:

"Good start. Now make it more concise / add specific examples / adjust the tone to be more [X]"
Output Format Control

Specify exactly how you want the output structured:

"Format your response as: - A one-sentence summary - Three bullet points with key details - A recommended next step"
